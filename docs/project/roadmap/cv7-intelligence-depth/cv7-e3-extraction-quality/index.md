# CV7.E3 — Extraction Quality

**Epic:** Memory extracted from conversations is fewer, sharper, deduplicated; descriptors for routing are generated and curated
**Status:** Done
**Depends on:** CV7.E1 (so extraction changes are eval-measurable), CV7.E2 (so extraction sees richer per-turn signals)
**Prerequisite for:** CV7.E4 (search/reinforcement need clean signal)

---

## What This Is

The extraction prompt in `src/memory/intelligence/extraction.py` is one of
the oldest pieces of the mirror and has not been seriously revised since
CV0. It is single-pass: one LLM call sees the transcript and emits 0–5
typed memories. Three failure modes accumulate:

- **Low recall on long conversations.** The model loses signal late in the
  transcript and produces fewer/weaker memories.
- **Low precision.** "0 to 5" lets the model extract trivial observations
  to fill a quota.
- **No deduplication.** Each conversation is extracted in isolation. Over
  time, near-duplicate memories pile up and dilute search.

This epic invests in extraction quality on three axes:

- **Prompt revision.** Sharper rules: prefer fewer high-quality memories
  over many trivial ones; layer/type discipline; explicit negative examples;
  shadow-aware extraction so `tension`/`pattern` material actually lands in
  `layer=shadow` instead of being routed to `ego` by default.
- **Two-pass extraction.** Candidate pass produces proposals; curate pass
  deduplicates against the user's existing memory pool (compact descriptors,
  not full text), decides final layer/type, merges or discards.
- **Generated descriptors.** Personas, journeys, and identity layers get
  short routing-optimized summaries generated by a cheap model. Reception
  reads the summaries, not full content. Routing accuracy goes up; content
  quality and routing quality decouple.

---

## Done Condition

- the extraction prompt is revised with eval-driven discipline (per-CV7.E1
  fixtures locking the new behavior)
- a two-pass extraction pipeline exists: `extract_candidates()` →
  `curate_against_existing()`; the curator receives a compact view of
  existing memories tagged similarly and decides merge/keep/drop
- per-conversation summary (3–4 sentences) is produced alongside memories
  and stored on the conversation row; used by `mm-recall` and as
  reranking input for memory search
- generated descriptors exist for personas and journeys (and the `self`
  and `ego` layers); reception reads them as the candidate-list input
- the extraction eval (CV7.E1.S2) shows higher quality scores on the same
  fixtures vs the CV6 baseline
- shadow-relevant material (tension, pattern, avoidance-shaped content)
  lands in `layer=shadow` reliably (eval coverage)

## Sequencing

```text
S1 (prompt revision, single-pass)
  └── S2 (two-pass with dedup)
S3 (per-conversation summary) — independent of S1/S2
S4 (generated descriptors) — depends on the descriptor storage decision
                              (open question, resolved during S4 planning)
```

S1 first because revising the prompt while still single-pass is faster to
iterate and the eval surface is simpler. S2 builds on S1's output shape and
adds the dedup loop. S3 is independent and can ship in parallel. S4 is the
biggest decision (storage shape — column on identity or sidecar table) and
benefits from the other three having shipped to inform what descriptors
actually need to carry.
